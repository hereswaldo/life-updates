Life Update:
PRD v0.2 · concept exploration · May 2026

Written by: Alex and Elisa, possibly on a couch

Status: Pre-build. Still has a placeholder name.

Audience: Us, next week's us, and whoever joins as co-founder #3

What we're making

People hate social media. People love their friends. There's a gap between those two facts and we're trying to live in it.

You post once on a schedule you pick. We grab your top song, the book you finished, your workouts, your photos, the show you watched, plus whatever you write in for restaurants, recipes, trips, and life updates. Your friends read it. They comment. You close the app. Everyone goes outside.

Who it's for

People in their late 20s to early 40s who deleted Instagram and then quietly missed knowing what their friends were up to. Starting with the Denver crew because they're nearby and they'll tell us when something's broken.

What we've already agreed on

  • iOS-only for v1. Apple is doing too much heavy lifting to also build for Android.
  • Invite-only. Friends invite friends. No public sign-ups.
  • Apple Sign In as the front door. Nobody wants another password.
  • You pick the cadence: weekly, monthly, or quarterly.
  • We will not auto-publish for you. Ever. Even if you ghost us.
  • Comment notifications are real-time (with sensible limits, see decision 03).
  • Discovery uses structured filters. The LLM can wait its turn.
  • The editor works even if you connect zero integrations. Type into a box like it's 2005.
  • Post titles, custom sections, restaurant and recipe presets all in v1.
  • No ads. We mean it.
  • Three people at peak. $300k/mo target. Lifestyle business with delusions of grandeur.
  • The name is Life Update: (yes, the colon is part of it).

What we still have to figure out

Ten things we have opinions about but haven't committed to. The recommendation is what we'd pick if we had to lock it tomorrow.

01
Push notification timing
When does the "your draft is ready" push fire? Default is the 1st of the month at 9am local. We could also let people pick a time.
Options: Fixed 9am local. Fixed but user-configurable hour. Smart-time based on when they usually open the app. Friday night because that's when people actually have time.
Our take: Fixed 9am local for v1. Add a "send me my draft on..." picker in v2 if anyone complains. Don't overengineer the first push.
02
To like or not to like
We started at "no likes ever." We flexed. We came back. This decision will get reopened at 2am in year two by whoever's looking at the engagement chart.
Options: Nothing. Hearts on posts. Hearts on comments only. A "saw this" eyeball that isn't a like but really is.
Our take: Stay at zero. The whole point is that you close the app. Likes are the doorway back to opening it fourteen times a day.
03
How loud the comment bell rings
Real-time is good. Real-time times forty-seven on a popular post is a hostage situation.
Options: Every comment pushes. First three then quiet. Twice-daily digest. User-controlled per post.
Our take: First three comments push. Everything else lands in a small badge. You hear from people. You don't get hunted.
04
How many photos
We picked five because Instagram picked ten and we wanted to feel smaller. That's not really a reason.
Options: Three (museum). Five (current, magazine spread). Ten (yearbook).
Our take: Stay at five. Five forces you to curate. If you can't pick five, you had a quiet month and that's also fine.
05
One feed or many circles
Right now everyone you follow sees everything. This is the "Sarah-from-college and Sarah-from-the-wedding-party" problem waiting to happen.
Options: One feed in v1, circles in v2. Circles in v1. Per-section visibility (workouts to gym crew, life updates to inner ring).
Our take: One feed for v1. Solves the cold start. We'll know in six months if anyone actually asks for circles.
06
Can you edit or delete after publishing
You will publish a typo. You will publish a recommendation that turns out to be food poisoning. You will publish a sentence that sounded better in your head.
Options: Locked forever (the Twitter philosophy). Editable for 24h. Always editable. Delete-only.
Our take: Edit window of 24h, delete anytime. Let people fix typos. Don't let them rewrite history.
07
When we ask people for money
Free forever is a charity. Paywall on day one is a vibe killer. The friend graph is the moat. We need it dense before we ask for the credit card.
Options: Free for v1 then freemium. Freemium from day one with a 3-integration cap. Founding member tier ($30/year for the first 1,000). Pay-what-you-want.
Our take: Free for v1. Open a founding member tier at 1,000 daily actives. Real paid tier at 10K MAU.
08
The Android question
Apple Photos and Apple Health are carrying water in this design. Cross-platform makes that hard, and a different team, and a different conversation entirely.
Options: iOS forever. Android after PMF. Android within six months. Launch both.
Our take: iOS-only until we cross $1M ARR. Then we have a conversation. Then probably still no.
09
Where new people come from after Denver
Invite-only is great. Invite-only forever means you have a friend group, not a business.
Options: Pure invite chains for 12 months. Paid acquisition after we know what works. Press push at 5K users. Partner with a digital wellness brand.
Our take: Pure invite chains for the first year. If word of mouth can't get us to 50K, we don't have a product, we have a hobby. Useful either way.
10
What happens to your draft if you ghost the deadline
We agreed no auto-publish. So what does the draft do? Sit there? Forever? Until the next month overwrites it?
Options: Draft sits indefinitely. Draft archives privately after 30 days. Draft expires and disappears. Draft becomes a journal entry only you can see.
Our take: Draft persists. Next month's data refreshes the auto-pulled sections but keeps anything you wrote in. No data loss. Move on with your life.

Things that could kill us

The graveyard
Path, BeReal, Poparazzi, Dispo. The anti-addiction social cemetery is large. Most died from being too thin or too punishing. We have to be useful before we're vibey.
The 29 empty days
If everyone posts on the 1st, days 2 through 30 are a ghost town. We're betting Discovery and comments fill the gap. If they don't, the app feels dead.
APIs are landlords
Goodreads is gone. Beli has no door. Google Photos broke in March 2025. Letterboxd will reject us politely. Spotify, Strava, and Hardcover are stable today and could change tomorrow. We should own as few of these as we can.
Real-time notifications are a trojan horse
We added them because they make the product feel alive. They also undermine the pitch. If DAU climbs past "once a month," we built a different product than we set out to build. Watch this number.
Friend graph density is everything
Three friends on the app means a sad feed. Thirty friends means the best thing on your phone. The product is a function of who else is already there.

How we'll know it's working

Did people actually post? 70%+
Was anyone there to read it? 8+ posts/mo
Did they come back next month? 60% retention
Did Discovery do anything? 4+ searches/mo
Will they pay us? 5% conversion

What we're not building yet

  • Android (it's an iPhone app, and we said so).
  • A web app (use your phone like a normal person).
  • DMs. iMessage exists. Use it.
  • Stories. We are not doing stories.
  • Ads. No, also no.
  • Public discoverable accounts. This is for your friends.
  • Verified badges. We believe you're real.
  • Brand accounts. Brands have enough places to be.
  • An algorithmic feed. Chronological is the feature.

What we do next

  • Show this prototype to the Denver crew. Watch their faces while they click.
  • Buy lifeupdate.app and lock @lifeupdate on every platform before someone else clocks it.
  • Email Letterboxd. They'll say no. Trakt is fine.
  • Apple Developer account. Three minutes and ninety-nine dollars.
  • Spec the OAuth flows for the five integrations that actually work.
  • Engineering scope: how long to build the editor and the feed? That number tells us whether this is a six-month thing or an eighteen-month thing.
